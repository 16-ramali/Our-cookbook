A popular Indian-Chinese dish made with paneer (Indian cheese), bell peppers, onions, and a spicy sauce.

Ingredients:

- Paneer: 250 gm, cut into small cubes
- Bell peppers: 2, cut into small pieces (any color)
- Onions: 2, cut into small pieces
- Ginger: 1-inch piece, minced
- Garlic: 3-4 cloves, minced
- Chilly sauce: 2-3 tbsp
- Soy sauce: 1-2 tbsp
- Vinegar: 1 tbsp
- Cornflour: 1-2 tbsp
- Salt: to taste
- Black pepper: to taste
- Oil: for frying

Instructions:

1. Prepare the sauce: Mix chilly sauce, soy sauce, vinegar, and cornflour in a bowl. Add a little water to make a smooth paste.
2. Marinate the paneer: In a separate bowl, mix salt, black pepper, and a pinch of cornflour. Add the paneer cubes and toss to coat evenly.
3. Fry the paneer: Heat oil in a wok or deep frying pan. Fry the paneer cubes until golden brown. Remove and set aside.
4. Stir-fry the vegetables: In the same wok, add more oil if needed. Stir-fry the bell peppers, onions, ginger, and garlic until the vegetables are tender-crisp.
5. Add the sauce: Pour the prepared sauce into the wok. Stir-fry for 1-2 minutes, until the sauce thickens.
6. Combine the paneer and sauce: Add the fried paneer cubes to the sauce. Stir-fry for another minute, until the paneer is well coated with the sauce.
7. Serve: Serve the paneer chilly hot, garnished with chopped green onions and a sprinkle of sesame seeds (optional).

Tips and Variations:

- Adjust the level of spiciness to your liking by adding more or less chilly sauce.
- Add a few drops of lemon juice for a tangy flavor.
- Use different colors of bell peppers for a colorful presentation.
- Add some chopped mushrooms, carrots, or baby corn to make the dish more nutritious and flavorful.

Enjoy your delicious homemade paneer chilly!
