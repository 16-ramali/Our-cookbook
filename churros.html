A classic Spanish dessert made with crispy fried dough pastries coated in cinnamon sugar, typically served with a side of rich chocolate
or caramel sauce for dipping.

Ingredients:

- All-purpose flour: 2 1/4 cups
- Baking powder: 1 tsp
- Salt: 1/2 tsp
- Granulated sugar: 1/3 cup
- Whole milk: 1 cup
- Large eggs: 2
- Vegetable oil: for frying
- Cinnamon sugar: for coating (see below for mixture)
- Chocolate or caramel sauce: for dipping (optional)
Cinnamon Sugar Mixture: - Granulated sugar: 1/2 cup
- Ground cinnamon: 2-3 tsp

Instructions:

1. Combine dry ingredients: In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, and salt.
2. Combine wet ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the sugar, milk, and eggs.
3. Combine wet and dry ingredients: Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until a smooth dough forms.
4. Knead the dough: Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes, until it is smooth and elastic.
5. Rest the dough: Cover the dough with plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.
6. Heat the oil: Heat the vegetable oil in a deep frying pan to 375°F (190°C).
7. Pipe the dough: Pipe the dough through a star tip into long, thin strips.
8. Fry the churros: Fry the churros for 2-3 minutes on each side, until they are golden brown and crispy.
9. Drain and coat: Remove the churros from the oil and place them on a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil. Coat the churros in cinnamon sugar mixture.
10. Serve: Serve the churros warm, with a side of chocolate or caramel sauce for dipping (if desired).

Tips and Variations:

- Use a thermometer to ensure the oil reaches the correct temperature.
- Don't overcrowd the frying pan, as this can lower the oil temperature and affect the texture of the churros. - Experiment with different flavors, such as adding a pinch of nutmeg or cardamom to the dough. - For a lighter coating, use powdered sugar instead of cinnamon sugar.
