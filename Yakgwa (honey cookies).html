Yakgwa (Honey Cookies)

Ingredients

Dough:

- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- ¼ tsp salt
- 3 tbsp sesame oil
- ½ cup honey
- ¼ cup soju (or water)

Frying:

- 2 cups vegetable oil

Syrup:

- ½ cup honey
- ½ cup water
- ¼ cup sugar
- 1 tsp cinnamon (optional)

Garnish:

- Toasted sesame seeds or pine nuts

Procedure

1️ Make the Dough

1. Mix flour & salt, then rub in sesame oil until crumbly.
2. Add honey & soju (or water), mixing into a soft dough.
3.Let it rest for 30 minutes.

2️ Shape the Cookies

1. Roll out dough (~¼ inch thick).
2.Cut into flower or square shapes & press a fork in the center.

3️ Fry Until Golden

1. Heat oil to 250°F (120°C) and fry slowly for 10-12 min until golden.
2. Drain on paper towels.

4️ Coat with Honey Syrup

1. Boil honey, water, sugar & cinnamon for 2-3 min, then cool slightly. 2. Soak fried cookies in syrup for at least 1 hour.

5️ Garnish & Serve

1. Sprinkle sesame seeds or pine nuts on top.
2. Enjoy with tea! 🍵


Serving Suggestions:

- Traditional Style: Serve with a warm cup of Korean tea (e.g., green tea, jujube tea, or barley tea).
- Gift Idea: Pack in a decorative box as a homemade gift.
- Modern Twist: Serve with vanilla ice cream for a sweet contrast.
- Dessert Platter: Pair with other Korean sweets like Tteok (rice cakes) or Hoddeok (sweet pancakes).
- Western Fusion: Enjoy with coffee or milk for a unique flavor pairing.


Tips and Variations:

Tips for Perfect Yakgwa:

- Use Sesame Oil for Authentic Flavor – It enhances the aroma and texture.
- Rest the Dough – This helps create a soft, chewy texture.
- Low & Slow Frying – Keep the oil temperature around 250°F (120°C) for even cooking.
- Let Yakgwa Soak Properly – The longer it soaks in the syrup, the richer the flavor (at least 1 hour).
- Store Properly – Keep in an airtight container at room temp for up to a week or refrigerate for longer freshness.
