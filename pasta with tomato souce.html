A classic Italian dish made with pasta, homemade tomato sauce, and parmesan cheese.

Ingridents:

- Pasta: 8 oz, spaghetti or penne work well
- Tomato sauce: (see below for recipe)
- Parmesan cheese: 1 cup, grated
- Fresh basil leaves: chopped, for garni
- Salt: to taste
- Freshly ground black pepper: to taste

Tomato Sauce Recipe:
- Canned crushed tomatoes: 2 cups
- Olive oil: 2 tbsp
- Onion: 1 medium, finely chopped
- Garlic: 3 cloves, minced
- Salt: to taste
- Freshly ground black pepper: to taste
- Dried basil: 1 tsp
- Dried oregano: 1 tsp

Instructions:

1. Cook the pasta: Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water before draining.
2. Make the tomato sauce: Heat the olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until translucent, about 3-4 minutes. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional minute.
3. Add the crushed tomatoes: Add the canned crushed tomatoes, salt, black pepper, dried basil, and dried oregano to the saucepan. Stir to combine.
4. Simmer the sauce: Reduce heat to low and simmer the sauce for 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has thickened and reduced slightly.
5. Combine the pasta and sauce: Add the cooked pasta to the tomato sauce and toss to combine, adding some reserved pasta water if the sauce seems too thick.
6. Top with parmesan cheese: Transfer the pasta to a serving dish and top with grated parmesan cheese.
7. Garnish with basil: Garnish with chopped fresh basil leaves and serve immediately.

Tips and Variations:

- Use high-quality ingredients, including canned San Marzano tomatoes and fresh parmesan cheese.
- Add protein like cooked ground beef, sausage, or chicken to make the dish more substantial.
- Mix in some chopped bell peppers, mushrooms, or olives to add extra flavor and nutrients.
- Use different types of pasta, such as linguine or rigatoni, to change up the texture and presentation.
- Add a sprinkle of red pepper flakes for some added heat.
