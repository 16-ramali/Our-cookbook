A classic Indian sweet made with cashews, sugar, and ghee, shaped into thin diamond-shaped slices.

Ingredients:

- Cashews: 1 cup
- Sugar: 1 cup
- Ghee: 1/4 cup
- Cardamom powder: 1/2 tsp
- Water: 1-2 tbsp
- Silver leaf (optional): for garnish

Instructions:

1. Grind the cashews: Soak the cashews in water for 30 minutes. Drain and grind them into a fine paste using a blender or food processor.
2. Make the sugar syrup: Combine the sugar and water in a saucepan. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar dissolves.
3. Add the cashew paste: Add the ground cashew paste to the sugar syrup. Cook, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and turns pale golden.
4. Add ghee and cardamom: Add the ghee and cardamom powder to the mixture. Cook for another minute, stirring constantly.
5. Shape the kaju katli: Pour the mixture onto a greased surface or a piece of parchment paper. Allow it to cool and set slightly.
6. Cut into diamond shapes: Use a sharp knife or a cookie cutter to cut the kaju katli into thin diamond shapes.
7. Garnish with silver leaf (optional): If desired, garnish the kaju katli with silver leaf for a luxurious touch.

Tips and Variations:

- Use high-quality cashews for the best flavor and texture.
- Adjust the amount of sugar to your taste.
- Add a pinch of saffron or a few drops of rosewater for added flavor and aroma.
- Experiment with different shapes and designs for the kaju katli.
