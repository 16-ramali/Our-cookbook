Kimchi Fried Rice (Kimchi Bokkeumbap)

Ingredients

Main Ingredients:

- 2 cups cooked rice (preferably day-old rice)
- 1 cup kimchi (chopped, with some juice)
- ½ cup protein (spam, ham, bacon, tofu, or chicken) (optional)
- 1 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste) (adjust to taste)
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp sesame oil
- 1 tbsp cooking oil
- 1 green onion (chopped)
- 1 tsp sugar (optional, balances acidity)

Toppings:

- 1 fried egg (sunny-side-up)
- Sesame seeds & more chopped green onions
- Roasted seaweed (gim/nori), shredded

Procedure

1️ Sauté Protein & Kimchi

1.Heat cooking oil in a pan over medium heat.
2.Add protein (if using) and cook until lightly browned.
3.Stir in kimchi and cook for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.

2️ Season & Add Rice

1. Add gochujang, soy sauce, and sugar. Stir well.
2. Add rice and mix everything together until evenly coated.
3. Stir-fry for 3-5 minutes, letting the rice get slightly crispy.

3️ Finish & Serve

1. Drizzle sesame oil and give a final stir.
2.Top with a fried egg, sesame seeds, green onions, and seaweed.

Enjoy!


Serving Suggestions:

- Classic Style: Serve with a fried egg on top & roasted seaweed strips.
- Korean Meal: Pair with miso soup or Korean fish cake soup.
- Extra Spicy: Add a drizzle of sriracha or more gochujang.
- Cheesy Twist: Melt mozzarella on top for a creamy flavor.
- Refreshing Side: Serve with cold cucumber salad or pickled radish.
- Protein Boost: Top with grilled bulgogi or stir-fried tofu.


Tips and Variations:

- Use Aged Kimchi – Older, fermented kimchi gives the best deep, tangy flavor.
- Day-Old Rice is Best – Slightly dry, leftover rice prevents mushy fried rice.
- Let It Crisp Up – Press the rice down for 30 seconds before stirring to get a crispy texture.
- Balance the Flavors – If too sour, add a bit of sugar; if too bland, add more kimchi juice or soy sauce.
- Customize It – Add your favorite protein (bacon, spam, tofu) or veggies (peas, corn, mushrooms).

Fun Variations:

- Spicy Kick – Add extra gochujang or chopped chili peppers.
- Cheesy Kimchi Fried Rice – Melt mozzarella on top for a creamy twist.
- Korean-Style Omurice – Wrap the fried rice in a thin egg omelet.
- Bacon Kimchi Fried Rice – Crispy bacon bits add extra umami.
- Vegetarian Version – Skip the meat and use mushrooms or tofu.
