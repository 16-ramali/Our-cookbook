A traditional Indian sweet flatbread made with a filling of cooked chickpeas, jaggery, and spices, wrapped in a whole wheat flour dough.

Ingredients:

For the dough:
 - Whole wheat flour: 2 cups
- Ghee or oil: 1/4 cup
- Salt: a pinch
- Water: as needed

For the filling:
 - Split chickpeas (chana dal): 1 cup
- Jaggery: 1 cup, grated
- Ghee or oil: 2 tbsp
- Cardamom powder: 1 tsp
- Nutmeg powder: 1/2 tsp
- Salt: a pinch

Instructions:

1. Make the dough: Mix the whole wheat flour, ghee or oil, and salt in a bowl. Gradually add water to form a smooth dough. Knead for 5-7 minutes. Cover and let rest for 30 minutes.
2. Make the filling: Cook the split chickpeas in water until they are soft. Drain and mash. Heat the ghee or oil in a pan. Add the grated jaggery and cook until it dissolves. Add the mashed chickpeas, cardamom powder, nutmeg powder, and salt. Mix well.
3. Assemble the puran poli: Divide the dough into 6-8 equal portions. Roll out each portion into a thin circle. Place a tablespoon or two of the filling in the center of each circle. Fold the dough over the filling to form half-moon shape. Press the edges to seal.
4. Cook the puran poli: Heat a non-stick pan or griddle over medium heat. Cook the puran poli for 1-2 minutes on each side, until they are golden brown.
5. Serve: Serve the puran poli warm or at room temperature. Traditionally, they are served with a dollop of ghee on top.

Tips and Variations:

- Use a mixture of whole wheat flour and all-purpose flour for a softer puran poli.
- Add a pinch of turmeric powder to the dough for a yellow color.
- Use different types of sugar, like brown sugar or coconut sugar, for a different flavor.
- Add some chopped nuts or dried fruit to the filling for added texture and flavor.
