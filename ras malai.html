A classic Indian dessert consisting of creamy paneer (Indian cheese) soaked in a sweet and fragrant milk syrup, flavored
with cardamom, saffron, and nuts.

Ingredients:

For the paneer:
 - Milk: 1 liter
- Lemon juice: 1-2 tbsp
- Cheesecloth or a clean cotton cloth: for straining
For the milk syrup:
 - Milk: 1 liter
- Sugar: 1 cup
- Cardamom powder: 1 tsp
- Saffron threads: 1/2 tsp, soaked in 1 tbsp hot water
- Chopped nuts (pistachios, almonds, or cashews): 1/4 cup
- Rosewater (optional): 1-2 tbsp

Instruction:

1. Make the paneer: Boil the milk in a large saucepan. Reduce the heat and add the lemon juice. Stir gently until the milk curdles and separates into curds and whey.
2. Strain the paneer: Line a strainer with cheesecloth or a clean cotton cloth. Carefully pour the curds and whey into the strainer. Let it drain for 30-40 minutes, until most of the liquid has been removed and the paneer has reached the desired consistency.
3. Prepare the milk syrup: Combine the milk, sugar, cardamom powder, and saffron (with its soaking liquid) in a saucepan. Heat over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the sugar dissolves and the mixture thickens slightly.
4. Add the paneer to the milk syrup: Cut the paneer into small cubes or slices. Add them to the milk syrup and simmer for 5-7 minutes, until the paneer is fully coated in the syrup.
5. Garnish with nuts and rosewater (if using): Remove the saucepan from the heat. Stir in the chopped nuts and rosewater (if using).
6. Chill and serve: Let the ras malai cool to room temperature. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight. Serve chilled.

Tips and Variations:

- Use high-quality milk and paneer for the best flavor and texture.
- Adjust the amount of sugar and spices to your taste.
- Add a few drops of kewra essence or a pinch of salt for added flavor.
- Experiment with different types of nuts or dried fruits for garnish.
